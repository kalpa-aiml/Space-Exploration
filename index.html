Space Missions

Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the first mission that landed humans on the Moon.It was launched by NASA on July 16, 1969.Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the Moon.Neil Armstrong said the famous words, “That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.”The mission safely returned to Earth on July 24, 1969.

Mars Rover

A Mars Rover is a robotic vehicle that explores the surface of Mars.It is sent by scientists to study the planet and collect data.Rovers take pictures, analyze rocks, and search for signs of water or life.One famous rover is Perseverance Rover, launched by NASA in 2020.Mars rovers help scientists learn more about the history and environment of Mars.

James Webb Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope is the most powerful space telescope ever built.It was launched on December 25, 2021 by NASA with international partners.The telescope studies distant galaxies, stars, and planets in deep space.It uses infrared technology to see objects that are very far away or hidden by dust.The James Webb Telescope helps scientists learn more about the early universe and the formation of stars and planets.

Voyager 1

Voyager 1 is a space probe launched by NASA in 1977.It was designed to study the outer planets of the solar system.mVoyager 1 sent important images and data about Jupiter and Saturn.It is the farthest human-made object from Earth.Today, Voyager 1 is traveling through interstellar space beyond our solar system.
